Fraudulent emails often have the below characteristics:
  • Fake Domain: This email was sent from a look-alike domain - itsecurity@rnjhlifesciences.com. This is a technique called typo-squatting. Phishing campaigns that target users in an organization often use this to try and trick users into clicking links, downloading file, and/or entering credentials.
  • Pressure Tactics: Phishing emails often try pressure tactics, like Security Alert, or "too good to be true opportunities", to motivate you to respond.
  • Evil Attachments: Scammer like to add attachments to emails. These attachments look like normal documents but contain malicious code that will grant them access onto your system.
  • Evil Links:Scammer also use links to get you to download evil documents or the link could take you to an already compromised site that can be used against you.
